Human+AI Collaboration Roles

A thinking tool, not rules: This framework helps you decide how to collaborate with AI based on your learning objectives. Use the quadrant to see your options, and the decision tree to find which approach fits your current situation.

Preserve agency: You maintain control over goals and decisions. The framework suggests approaches, but you choose what works for your context, constraints, and values.

Prerequisites: This model assumes ethical, legal, environmental, etc. concerns have been addressed and appropriate tools selected. It focuses solely on learning value when selecting collaboration approaches.

Key Concepts

  • Learning First: Educational goals drive the collaboration strategy.
  • Cognitive Delegation: Intentionally dividing intellectual tasks between human and AI based on learning goals.
  • Dynamic Roles: Fluidly switch roles throughout a project as needed, even within a single task!.

Application Modes

Diagnostic (Reflecting):

"Which approach am I currently using? Does it align with my learning goals?"

Example: Teacher realizes they're always letting AI lead—missing opportunities for deeper student thinking.

Prescriptive (Planning):

"Given my objectives and constraints, which approach should I adopt?"

Example: For creative writing unit, start with Human-Led Exploration, then shift to Human-Led Application.

4 Levels of AI Abstraction

Understanding AI at different levels:

✓ Behavioral: How AI acts in collaboration
✓ "Cognitive": What AI "knows" and how it "thinks"
✗ Conceptual: Math & programming (not needed)
✗ Physical: Hardware & infrastructure (not needed)

Probing & Perspective-Taking

Why Probe? Testing AI's understanding helps you calibrate your collaboration approach.

Perspective-Taking: Understanding what AI "knows" requires cognitive empathy—imagining how it processes information differently from humans.

Think of AI as a "biased but productive intern"—eager to help but needs clear direction and verification.

All DPVR skills require perspective-taking to be effective.

Design Process & Collaboration Modes

The Double Diamond maps to our collaboration framework:

  • Discover & Develop (divergent thinking) → Exploring axis
    Generating possibilities, whether Human-Led or AI-Led
  • Define & Deliver (convergent thinking) → Applying axis
    Making decisions and executing, whether Human-Led or AI-Led

Each diamond phase can use any quadrant approach:

  • Discover: Often Human-Led Exploration for deep understanding
  • Define: Human-Led Application to synthesize insights
  • Develop: AI-Led Exploration for rapid option generation
  • Deliver: AI-Led Application for efficient execution

You'll cycle through multiple diamonds in a project, fluidly switching approaches based on learning goals!
