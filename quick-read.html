Human+AI Collaboration Roles

Cognitive Delegation with Agency

What is this: A practical tool for deciding how humans and AI should collaborate. No technical knowledge required—just understand how AI behaves, not how it's coded.

What isn't this: Not prescriptive rules. Not a yes/no to AI. Not about replacing human creativity. It's a thinking tool that preserves student agency while teaching AI collaboration.

Key Concepts

  • Cognitive Delegation: Intentionally dividing intellectual tasks. Each role delegates differently—exploration, execution, gathering, or building.
  • Perspective-Taking: Understanding what AI "knows" and how it "thinks"
  • Dynamic Role-Trading: Switch roles as project needs change
  • Student Agency: Learner maintains control over goals and decisions
  • Learning Objectives First: Choose role based on what students need to learn

Discovery ↔ Delivery

Discovery: Divergent thinking, exploring possibilities, understanding problems

Example: Brainstorming essay topics, researching perspectives, exploring solutions

Delivery: Convergent thinking, executing plans, producing outputs

Example: Writing final draft, creating presentation, solving specific problems

Human-Led ↔ AI-Led

Human-Led: You set direction & milestones, AI supports your vision

Example: Student creates outline, AI helps polish language

AI-Led: AI generates options & drafts, you evaluate & guide

Example: AI creates practice problems, student selects appropriate ones

4 Levels of AI Abstraction

✓ Level 3 - Behavioral: How AI acts and reacts in dynamic collaboration
✓ Level 2 - "Cognitive": Using perspective-taking to understand what AI "knows" and how it "thinks"
✗ Level 1 - Conceptual: Math & programming under the hood (not covered here)
✗ Level 0 - Physical: Hardware & infrastructure requirements (not covered here)

Application Modes

Diagnostic (Reflecting):

"Which role am I currently playing? Does it align with my learning goals?"

Example: Teacher realizes they're always in Orchestrator mode—missing opportunities for deeper student thinking.

Prescriptive (Planning):

"Given my objectives and constraints, which role should I adopt?"

Example: For creative writing unit, start as Strategist (brainstorming), then shift to Implementer (drafting).
